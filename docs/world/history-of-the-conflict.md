# History of the Conflict in Isger

_Player-safe summary for characters at the start of the campaign._

For immediate scene-one context, pair this with `md/campaign-start-briefing.md`.

## The Long Shadow Over Isger

Isger has spent most of its history under outside rule. Taldor conquered the land first, and Cheliax later annexed it and turned it into a vassal state. Since then, many Isgeri have felt their country exists to serve foreign rulers, merchants, and military planners rather than its own people. That feeling is strongest outside Elidir, where survival has often depended more on neighbors, temples, and stubborn local communities than on any distant authority.

## The Goblinblood Wars

Modern Isger was shaped above all by the Goblinblood Wars. Violence out of the Chitterwood spread across the countryside, burning settlements, uprooting families, and leaving lasting fear behind. Steward Hedvend VI became a symbol of failure to many Isgeri, remembered as too slow or too concerned with protecting wealth and trade to defend ordinary people. The war ended only when several outside forces, including Hellknights and Eagle Knights, helped halt the devastation.

The peace that followed never felt whole. Veterans came home maimed, children were orphaned, and whole communities disappeared. Cheliax and the Church of Asmodeus later offered aid, and some people remain grateful for it. Others remember that help as control arriving after abandonment. That divided memory still shapes every argument about loyalty, rebellion, and order.

## A Country Caught in Other People's Wars

Isger's people know their homeland is often treated as a buffer, a route for trade, or a stage on which stronger nations settle their arguments. That fear has returned with new force since the Godsrain and the scattering of warshards. Across the Inner Sea, wars and uprisings are flaring, and many people now call the widening struggle between Cheliax and Andoran the Hellfire Crisis. Isger was where the spark landed, but it is no longer the only place feeling the heat. In that atmosphere, even ordinary people began to suspect that every major clash might also be about who would seize divine fragments and what power those fragments might unlock.

That wider crisis matters even to people far from the front. Cheliax has tightened its borders, the Aspodean Wall has become a symbol of open confrontation, and fighting at sea threatens trade throughout the Inner Sea. So when Isgeri speak about the current conflict, they are not only talking about local grudges. They are also talking about the risk that their country will once again be used by larger powers.

## The Forces People Recognize

Two foreign-aligned military traditions loom especially large in public memory. The Hellknights are widely feared as merciless enforcers of law, with faceless helms and a reputation for cruelty in the name of order. Some insist the Hellknights are independent, but to many ordinary people that distinction matters less than what their armor means when it appears in town.

The Eagle Knights carry a different reputation. They are Andoran's champions of freedom, diplomats, soldiers, and agitators against tyranny. In Isger, that makes them both inspiring and dangerous depending on whom you ask. Older people remember that Hellknights and Eagle Knights once fought on the same side during the Goblinblood Wars; now they stand on opposite sides of a new crisis.

## Breachill and the Breaking Point

The immediate crisis began in Breachill. The Order of the Rack occupied the town to root out dissidents and seize anything connected to the Hellbreakers League. Public punishments and executions turned the occupation into terror. When retired Andoren general Reginald Cormoth came as part of a diplomatic effort, the Hellknights executed him as well. News of his death spread quickly enough that it became common talk far beyond Breachill.

That killing transformed a local crackdown into a regional scandal. Eagle Knights entered Isger and fought the Battle of Hellknight Hill, joined by the Hellbreakers. Public memory of the battle was made even more vivid by reports that the diabolic dragon Kierotheax fought on Cheliax's side. The occupiers were defeated and Breachill was freed. Whatever people believe about rebellion, almost no one can now pretend the old order is stable.

## What War Feels Like on the Ground

For ordinary people, war is not just banners and speeches. It is broken wagons, patched walls, funerals, rationing, frightened villages, rumors of spies, and arguments over who is a patriot and who is a traitor. It creates soldiers and rebels, but also refugees, mechanics, carpenters, chaplains, smugglers, and orphans. Even people far from a battlefield feel its pressure in shortages, travel dangers, and the need to choose sides or at least appear to.

That is one reason the conflict in Isger feels so volatile at the start of the campaign. The Hellbreakers offer hope, Saringallow offers organization, and Breachill offers proof that resistance can win. But many Isgeri still remember what the last great war cost them, and they fear what another one might demand.

## Where Things Stand at the Start of the Campaign

At the beginning of this adventure, Isger is still under Chelish rule, but it is no longer quiet. The Hellbreakers have momentum. Saringallow has become a center of resistance. The countryside is full of old wounds, new courage, and deep uncertainty. Isger's people are asking whether freedom is finally possible and whether they can survive the price of trying to claim it.
