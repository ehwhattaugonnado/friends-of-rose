## ABOVE THE FOLD

# WAR FROM A SINGLE SPARK

### Hellknight execution, Eagle Knight intervention, and a vassal state's unrest threaten to set the Inner Sea aflame

## A BRIEF INTRODUCTION

The conflicts collectively known as the Hellfire Crisis were sparked by the execution of an Andoren diplomat by the Order of the Rack Hellknights, allegedly acting under Queen Abrogail Thrune's orders. Now, they've spiraled throughout the region. This reporter spent the past several months traversing Andoran, Cheliax, and Isger to bring a ground-level understanding of the Hellfire Crisis to you, the reader.

This series of collected dispatches, both penned by this reporter and gathered from other sources, is intended to provide a broader picture of the Crisis. This reporter has sought to not only solicit the accounts of notables who command armies, but also those of the "ordinary" people who are often most affected, yet least noticed, when the flames of war engulf the world around them.

Without further ado, let us begin where the Crisis started.

## FRONT PAGE LEAD

# HELLKNIGHT HILL FALLS

### Eagle Knights seize Breachill as rebel volunteers turn the tide

## EAGLE KNIGHTS TAKE BREACHILL

*Reported by Arianna Dreth*

BREACHILL, ISGER-The small town of Breachill, tucked in the foothills of eastern Isger, is an unlikely battleground, but a battleground it has become. Readers who have kept a keen eye on these pages will recall the Order of the Rack took control of the town due to allegations that the Hellbreakers League was using it as a base to hide warshards for use against Cheliax.

This reporter accompanied a unit of Eagle Knights, gaining a firsthand look at the battle-and what a battle it was! Fire blazed night and day over Hellknight Hill, where the Eagle Knights dug trenches to protect themselves during their offensive up its slopes. Citadel Altaerein's siege engines weren't the only thing these warriors had to contend with-the Hellknights had a powerful ally, a diabolic dragon. This dragon, Kierothonax, is well-known to have ties to Thrune, likely confirming that the Order of the Rack's operations in Breachill were sanctioned by Queen Abrogail. This reporter kept to the back lines, but even so, nearly got the hairs scorched off her neck by infernal flames.

Just when the Hellknights seemed to be gaining the upper hand, a volunteer force arrived to join the Eagle Knights, turning the tide. Chased out of their citadel, the Hellknights attempted a last-ditch assault on Breachill itself, but these new allies thwarted them. From within their war wagons, the remainder of the Hellknights broke through Andoren lines to escape, withdrawing toward Logas along with Kierothonax.

There are rumors that the Hellbreakers League and Andoren forces are engaging in talks, and an anonymous source relayed that the Hellbreakers have gifted the Eagle Knights a powerful warshard. In a statement, Supreme Elect Andira Marusek indicated that Eagle Knights traveling to Breachill to redress the issue were following their own consciences and not official directives.

There is some precedent for the Eagle Knights to protect Isger's interests as an independent party. The Hellknights and the Eagle Knights fought side-by-side to defend Isger during the Goblinblood Wars a generation ago. During that conflict, the king of Cheliax, then Inflexus Thrune, accepted the Eagle Knights' involvement in his vassal's territory on the condition of that very independence. Although no official claims have been made, the Eagle Knights in Breachill seem rather well-versed on this bit of legal history.

## OFFICIAL STATEMENT

# ALMAS DEMANDS JUSTICE

### People's Council condemns the killing of General Reginald Cormoth

## STATEMENT FROM THE PEOPLE'S COUNCIL OF ANDORAN

*Released by Supreme Elect Andira Marusek*

**ALMAS, ANDORAN**-The following statement was released following the emergency People's Council called to discuss the execution of General Reginald Cormoth in Isger. It has been reproduced here in its entirety.

"Citizens, neighbors, I stand before you today to relay the declaration of the People's Council regarding the execution of Retired General Reginald Cormoth. General Cormoth was a champion of the people and a good man. When it came to his attention that the residents of Breachill were under assault by the Hellknight Order of the Rack, who claimed on the flimsiest of evidence that anti-Chelaxian forces were conspiring within the town, he volunteered at once to lead a diplomatic retinue to Isger and negotiate peace.

"No doubt General Cormoth expected his mission of diplomacy to be greeted in the usual way, by opening channels of communication to discuss the issues. No doubt he believed the Order of the Rack, cruel though they may be, was constrained by the rule of law and would not attack an ambassador. Unfortunately, he was wrong.

"With no attempt to negotiate, the Order of the Rack falsely accused General Cormoth of unspecified 'crimes' against Chelaxian holdings and executed him, without trial or opportunity for appeal. We condemn, in the strongest of terms, this unlawful and barbaric action. The Order of the Rack has overstepped its authority not only in subjugating the town of Breachill, but in murdering an Andoren citizen.

"Should any member of the Order of the Rack who was involved in this crime set foot in Andoran, they will be immediately arrested to account for their actions. They will be given a fair trial-more than they offered General Cormoth-but anyone with a hand in this horrific murder will be brought to justice."

## WAR DESK

# CHELIAX ANSWERS WITH WAR

### Queen Abrogail mobilizes; Hellknight orders ordered beneath the Thrune banner

## CHELIAX DECLARES WAR ON ANDORAN, TAKES CONTROL OF HELLNIGHTS

*Reported by Arianna Dreth*

AUGUSTANA, ANDORAN-War has erupted on the shores of the Inner Sea, following months of escalating tensions between Cheliax and Andoran. With the Eagle Knights' victory at Breachill, Queen Abrogail Thrune has declared this incursion into Isgar as an act of war and begun mobilizing her forces. As hostilities appear inevitable, diplomatic attachés stationed in both countries are returning home. In Andoran, meanwhile, the People's Council voted by a significant margin to respond to Queen Abrogail's declaration in kind.

To support the war effort, Queen Abrogail has declared all Hellknight orders to be under her direct control, which has caused some consternation among non-Chelaxian orders. Lictor Severs "Boneclaw" DiViri of the Order of the Nail, based in Varisia, issued this statement in response: "The Order of the Nail is not a pawn to be used in petty national squabbles." Unsurprisingly, Lictor Octavio Sabinus of the Order of the Torrent in Ravounel also declined the queen's command, stating, "In spite of our name, Hellknights owe no fealty to Hell. The Torrent is no Chelaxian vassal, and we will not betray our compatriots who fought for independence to bend the knee to a tyrant."

Within Cheliax, the Order of the Scourge has splintered over the decree. Queen Abrogail elevated former Paralictor Ivo Elliendo to lead the order, alleging that Lictor Toulon Vidoc was collaborating with Cheliax's enemies to undermine her rule. Meanwhile, Vidoc, rumored to be investigating Queen Abrogail herself for potential malfeasance, has declared both Cheliax's takeover of the Hellknights and his own ouster to be unlawful. A sizable portion of the Order of the Scourge appears to have followed him, though the current whereabouts of Vidoc and his loyalists are unknown.

## THE WALL FRONT

# BASALT, BLOOD, AND BRICKWORK

### Cheliax completes the Aspodean Wall; Brastlewark dissent stirs beneath the stones

## AFTER YEARS, A WALL

*Reported by Arianna Dreth*

For over a decade, Queen Abrogail Thrune of Cheliax has sought the construction of a wall from the Aspodell Mountains to the Inner Sea. However, the gnomes of Brastlewark tasked with the assignment have added to the massive project in fits and starts, leaving a scattered patchwork of walls that did nothing to protect Cheliax or impede trade.

This reporter took little note as the gnomes resumed the project last year, but it seems now that Cheliax's full resources were behind them. With a powerful working of infernal magic, liquid stone filled in the gaps left by years of fitful work, hardening to complete the wall in basalt. The red-hot stone was visible for miles offshore as it cooled.

Cheliax's army has already begun moving to take advantage of the completed fortification, provoking a matching buildup on Andoran's side of the new wall. The Hellknights are also on the move, with the Order of the Rack moving knights from eastern Cheliax into Isger. Although independent from the Chelaxian throne, high-ranking sources suggest the Hellknights' actions are intended to reinforce Cheliax's hold over its vassal.

## ANDORAN CLASHES WITH BRASTLEWARK AT THE ASPODEAN WALL

*Reported by Arianna Dreth*

TIPTRIN, ANDORAN-Rising high enough to blot out the sky if one is near enough to the craggy peaks, the Aspodell Mountains are a formidable barrier between Andoran and Cheliax. However, the land to the south has long been contested. For years, Queen Abrogail Thrune has tasked the gnomes of Brastlewark with creating a wall suitable to defend the long border to the Inner Sea. Work continued in inconsistent fits and starts as the gnomes attempted a variety of plans, but the work is recently completed. With some sections completed by the workers' newfound vigor and others filled in with infernal stone, the wall stands patchwork but whole.

Upon making her way to the Arthfell, where a regiment of Andoren infantry and Eagle Knight Golden Legionnaires maintains a base camp for incursions against the wall, this reporter was greeted by Commander Azhafelle Pennerie, the camp's leader. Once Commander Pennerie was convinced this reporter was not a Chelaxian operative, she agreed to grant limited access to the camp and opened her collection of spices for a feast unbefitting an army camp. She declined an interview, however, indicating they needed to focus on the pass's defense.

In the ongoing struggle the Golden Legionnaires are calling the Battle of the Wall, no clear victor has yet emerged. From the protection of their massive new fortification, Cheliax has been launching waves of infantry attacks along with dispatching groups of imps and other infernal creatures into Andoran's heartland. Although Andoran has been able to fend off these assaults thus far, the wall itself stands strong.

According to reports within camp, fighting alongside the Chelaxians are contingents of gnome warriors from Brastlewark, directed by their king, Drum Thornfiddle, to maintain the wall. Interestingly, a group of several haggard gnomes recently arrived at the pass, undergoing some scrutiny before meeting with Pennerie. This reporter was unable to witness the conversation, but one of the gnomes, identifying himself only as "the Crow," agreed to speak with her afterward. "King Thornfiddle's willingness to play lackey to Abrogail has cost the people of Brastlewark dearly," he said. "If the king won't stand up to her, then we will." The Crow then winked at this reporter and offered her a jet-black feather before departing. It seems there may be dissent brewing in Brastlewark.

## SEA TRADE & DIPLOMACY

# STRAIT HELD, HARBORS WATCHED

### Corentyn tightens the noose while Absalom chooses neutrality

## CHELIAX TIGHTENS GRIP ON HESPERETH STRAIT, ABSALOM VOWS NEUTRALITY

Reported by Arianna Dreth

CORENTYN, CHELIAX-Corentyn, a military outpost grown into a trade hub, is called the City of Nine Forts, though this moniker drastically underestimates its defenses. Corentyn's position grants it firm control over the Hespereth Strait and passage out of the Inner Sea, which in peacetime it used to heavily tax travel. In wartime, the atmosphere has turned decidedly bellicose, and Chelaxian naval uniforms are on every corner.

As diplomats of both countries attempt to form alliances with other nations-Andoren attachés in the halls of Galt, Highhelm, Kyonin, and Ravounel; Chelaxian in Molthune and Nidal; and both vying for Taldor's and Druma's ears-Cheliax has tightened their hold on the strait, no doubt viewing it as a valuable bargaining tool. The Chelaxian blockade is allowing neutral ships to pass through, but stops-or fires on-any ships believed to be allied with Andoran.

Wynsal Starborn, Acting Primarch of Absalom, recently issued a statement of neutrality in the war, joining the governments of Osirion and Katapesh. Though the Acting Primarch cited an unwillingness to embroil the entire region in a conflict between two nations, it's likely that trade considerations played a part in the decision.

A question looms on the southern end of the strait. Rahadoum has made it clear they intend to enforce their ancestral claim on Khari. As their armies gather, it threatens to open another front Cheliax can ill-afford to protect, but also one vital to their diplomacy.

## RAHADOUM

# KHARI CLAIM RENEWED

### The Sword of Mortality invokes old wrongs and new war

## SWORD OF MORTALITY ISSUES KHARI STATEMENT

*Released by Karsakim*

SHEPHERD'S ROCK, RAHADOUN-The following was released by Karsakim, the Sword of Mortality, and the leader of Rahadoum's Pure Legion. Although Rahadoum's civilian leadership have repeated their claim on the city of Khari for centuries, Karsakim's statement seems particularly relevant today.

"The city of Khari and the surrounding territories were seized by Cheliax under the direction of the god Aroden. Despite the changes in Cheliax after his death, Asmodeus is no more willing to give mortals freedom than any god."

"Khari will once again be ruled under the Laws of Mortality, freed from the chains of Asmodeus and his puppets. Those who wish in their hearts to follow him are advised to find a new home on Chelaxian soil."

## LETTERS FROM THE FRONT

# "WE'RE FIGHTING FOR SOMETHING BIGGER THAN ANY OF US"

### A sailor's last letter reaches home

## A LETTER FROM THE FRONT

Written by Petty Officer Second Class Evalira Caldis, reprinted with permission of the Caldis family

Mother, Father,

I hope you're well. I'm sure you've heard about the attacks on Augustana's harbor, so I wanted to let you know I'm fine. The *Liberty's Feather* is on the move, although I can't tell you her destination, of course. We have good winds, though. I've been trying to keep up the spirits of the newer recruits; I think some of them are worried about fighting those devils. But we're well prepared, so don't you worry either! After we made it through our first battle, Captain Vicray said I might make lieutenant someday. That'd be something, wouldn't it?

Of course, I know it's dangerous. But we're fighting for something bigger than any of us. If Cheliax wants to make us all thralls to Hell, they'll have to get through our ships first. I do miss the sight of Souston's painted houses in the sunshine, though. I hope the battles haven't scared off all the fish. Say hello to Gerstan and Marna for me, too; maybe by the time the baby is born, our shores will be peaceful again.

All my love,
Evalira

**EDITOR'S NOTE:** Evalira Caldis was killed in a clash with Chelaxian naval forces while the *Liberty's Feather* ran the blockade of the Hespereth Strait on 18 Kuthona. Captain Mestrow Vicray posthumously promoted Caldis to lieutenant, as her heroism saved the lives of her fellow officers during a retreat from the *Dark Prince*. Her family offered her final letter for publication to this newspaper so her bravery wouldn't be forgotten.

## WHO IS CAUGHT IN THE FLAMES?

# THE POWERS NOW EMBROILED

### A quick guide for readers whose maps are already out of date

## NATIONS EMBROILED

Although the primary belligerents are Andoran and Cheliax, the Hellfire Crisis has spread throughout the Inner Sea. The following powers are the most embroiled.

**Andoran:** After splitting off from Cheliax around 50 years ago to pursue democratic ideals, Andoran remains bitter rivals with its former rulers.

**Cheliax:** Torn apart by decades of civil war, modern Cheliax is ruled by House Thrune and deeply influenced by their faith in Asmodeus.

**Isger:** The spark of war landed in this vassal state of Cheliax.

**Molthune:** This militaristic nation has gone wild with bloodlust, attempting to claim its lost territory of Nirmathas and conquer others.

**Nirmathas:** Trapped between Molthune and the undead forces of the Gravelands, Nirmathas calls out to its allies around Lake Encarthan.

**Nidal:** Wracked with internal division, this shadowy theocracy can't aid its allies in Cheliax.

**Rahadoum:** Rejecting all faiths, including that of Asmodeus, Rahadoum has directed its involvement at reclaiming lost territory from Cheliax.

**Ravounel:** Only recently freed from Chelaxian rule, Ravounel is doing what it can for rebels in nearby Nidal.

**Taldor:** This empire wistfully remembers controlling Andoran, Cheliax, Molthune, and others.

## BRIEF HISTORY FOR THE BREAKFAST TABLE

# HOW DID WE GET HERE?

### From Aroden's death to warshards over the Inner Sea

## BEFORE THE CRISIS

- 4606 AR** The god Aroden, prophesied to lead Cheliax into the Age of Glory, instead dies. The Age of Lost Omens begins.
- 4608 AR** House Thrune attempts to take control of Cheliax, beginning a brutal civil war.
- 4736 AR** Moltune takes advantage of the civil war to secede from Cheliax.
- 4640 AR** House Thrune takes control of Cheliax with support from Nidal and the powers of Hell itself. Worship of Asmodeus becomes the state religion.
- 4648 AR** Moltune splits further as its northern provinces rebel, which eventually become Nirmathas.
- 4669 AR** Andoran rises up after decades of infernal rule and breaks off from Cheliax.
- 4717 AR** The Archduchy of Ravounel breaks off from Cheliax, but a broader rebellion, known as the Glorious Reclamation, is suppressed.
- 4724 AR** The god Gorum dies. Shards of his armor, known as warshards, fall across the Inner Sea.
- 4725 AR** Nirmathas enters an alliance with most of its neighbors to defeat the undead of the Whispering Tyrant. Moltune abstains, attacking Nirmathas only months later.
- Cheliax completes the Aspodean Wall on its border with Andoran.
- A clash between Eagle Knights and Hellknights in Isger spirals into war between Andoran and Cheliax.
