# Session 002 — The Everbloom Imports Job

**Date:** 6/15/26

**Characters:** Edlo Zothus, Korrik Vesh, Tainis Falco, Yun Ze

**Duration:** ~1h 45m

**Locations:** Saringallow — Hellbreakers headquarters, Saringallow docks / warehouse district, Everbloom Imports warehouse, The Fortnight Inn (Saringallow)

**NPCs:** Gertraud Mildenhall, Chelaxian Loyalist Leader, Chelaxian Loyalists, Street-smart halfling urchin (unnamed), Rhokrag, Maire Phelan

**Transcript:** [Read the full session transcript →](transcripts/session-002.md)

> The party scoped out the Everbloom Imports warehouse, sprang a falling-crate trap, fought off the Chelaxian loyalists holding it, rescued Gertraud Mildenhall, learned the expected supply shipment never arrived, and accepted Rhokrag's next mission — a riverboat ambush.

## Session Events

### Briefing and departure

- The party awoke well-rested at the Hellbreakers' Saringallow headquarters (Korrik, Yun Ze, and Edlo all restored resources).
- Rhokrag had tasked them to investigate why a shipment never arrived at the League's dockside warehouse (Everbloom Imports) and why Gertraud Mildenhall, sent to receive it, hadn't reported back.
- The party chose to scope the warehouse district and ask around before approaching directly.

### Scoping the docks

- Edlo (Perception 25, secret) spotted a street-smart young halfling urchin watching the comings and goings near the warehouse.
- Tainis approached her; his Diplomacy roll was poor (8) and she was wary, but she agreed to talk for coin.
- Tainis paid her four silver. She reported: a couple of "nasty-looking blokes" forced their way in that morning without a key, a "nice halfling lady" went in earlier with her key, and nobody had come out since.

### Casing and entering the warehouse

- The party circled the warehouse: the loading doors had a heavy padlock; the smaller side door's lock showed signs of being forced.
- Edlo opened the side door and the party entered.

### The falling-crates trap

- A tripwire trap (Tumble Down, DC 20 Basic Reflex) dropped crates on the entrants. Edlo critically succeeded (nat 20, total 29). Yun Ze failed (12), took 26 bludgeoning damage, and dropped unconscious.
- Tainis used Treat Wounds (success) to revive Yun Ze for 8 HP.
- A group Perception check revealed the muffled sounds of a bound, gagged creature in the next room and the impression of several people present.

### The warehouse fight

- The party crept to the corner; Edlo spotted Gertraud Mildenhall bound and gagged on a crate, with a tall dark-skinned man (the Chelaxian Loyalist Leader) standing menacingly over her. The dropped crates had already alerted the occupants.
- Combatants: the four PCs plus Gertraud (fighting on the party's side once freed) vs. the Chelaxian Loyalist Leader and three Chelaxian Loyalists (one initially hidden).
- The fight was brutal and went badly at first: the Leader's heavy crossbow critically hit Edlo, and a hidden crossbowman dropped Edlo unconscious in round one.
- Korrik (barbarian, polearm) was the standout — repeated flanking critical hits, cleaving an enemy's arm off and finishing multiple foes.
- Yun Ze critically hit with Flurry of Blows and "annihilated" a loyalist (fist through the chest), then stepped over and cut Gertraud's bonds, freeing her.
- Tainis (now a bard) sustained Courageous Anthem, critically succeeded on Lingering Composition (anthem locked in for 4 rounds), and chipped in with Needle Darts and a sonic cantrip.
- Edlo went down a second time: a failed Battle Medicine on himself, then a Risky Surgery gamble that knocked him unconscious again.
- Gertraud, freed, threw a few (missed) punches and then caught her breath, too hurt to chase.
- Korrik and Yun Ze used Demoralize / Intimidating Glare to frighten the survivors; the last loyalist was knocked prone and finished by Korrik's Sudden Charge.
- All four loyalists died. Despite the GM noting a captive might have been worth questioning, Korrik killed the fleeing survivor. The whole party survived.

### Aftermath, loot, and Gertraud's account

- Gertraud thanked the party. She said she came to the warehouse, found the loyalists already there, and was captured. Critically: there were no supplies on the ship — the expected crates of weapons were never there.
- Gertraud gifted the party two trinkets (placed in the party stash): the Whispering Wire (magical comm device, Message-like) and the Masquerade Scarf (illusion disguise item). Tainis took the Masquerade Scarf.
- Loot collected: the Leader's heavy crossbow (Edlo kept it), plus two crossbows, two light maces, two sets of leather armor, and bolts. The party kept one crossbow and the bolts (Edlo took 20 bolts); the spare weapons and armor were given to Gertraud for the League.

### Reporting back

- The party returned to HQ ("friends of Rose"). Gertraud reported to Rhokrag.
- Rhokrag was concerned the loyalists had learned the warehouse's location (Gertraud had no explanation) and noted the diverted-supply request simply hadn't panned out.
- Maire gave each PC two gold and sent them to the Fortnight Inn to rest.

### Night at the Fortnight Inn

- Yun Ze and Tainis treated the party's wounds; nobody remained at risk.
- The party kept a low profile (wanted posters for Isger loyalists are common; Korrik conceals his hobgoblin features). The resident band, the Oasis Rain Showers, played. Tainis socialized quietly, Korrik watched from a booth, Yun Ze reflected in his room, Edlo walked the streets and met a friendly local.

### Next morning — new mission

- Rhokrag laid out the League's ethos: take only what's needed, never from those who can't afford it, and keep killing to a minimum to win the people of Isger over and deny Cheliax propaganda.
- He assigned the next mission: light piracy — ambush a Chelaxian-flagged cargo ship at a narrow, rocky river outcropping up-river (ships run cargo from Elidir in the north to Cheliax in the south) and bring it back to the warehouse.
- Rhokrag gave the group 10 gold for supplies. The session ended with planning deferred to next session; all conditions cleared and HP restored.

## Mechanics and Rules Notes

- Tainis Falco's build was rebuilt this session into a full **bard** (storyteller muse direction; plans toward Multifarious Muse / Warrior Muse later). Spells used: Courageous Anthem, Lingering Composition, Needle Darts, Soothe, plus a sonic cantrip. The Session 001 "may shift toward bard" note is now resolved.
- GM uses **milestone leveling** (no XP tracking); the party did not level this session (still level 1).
- GM hand-waves overnight HP recovery to full given the party's healer capability, barring a compelling reason.
- Rest mechanics: the in-VTT rest button only restores a small amount of HP naturally; the GM clears conditions and tops off HP at narrative rest points.
- Treat Wounds / Battle Medicine: a target is immune to a given healer's Treat Wounds/Battle Medicine for a day after use (Edlo became immune to both Tainis's and his own further attempts mid-fight).
- Risky Surgery: deals 1d8+1 self-damage for +2 to the Medicine check; can knock the user unconscious (it did, for Edlo).
- Flanking and status bonuses stack meaningfully (–2 AC from flanking plus +1 from Courageous Anthem turned a hit into Yun Ze's crit).
- Hero Points were used for rerolls throughout; each PC started with one.
- Heavy crossbow vs. crossbow: d10+1 vs. d6+1 damage, more range, but Reload 2 vs. Reload 1. Longbow is Reload 0.
- Secret rolls (Perception, Diplomacy) are rolled blind to the players by default in this game.
- Foundry setup hiccups: the dying module wasn't enabled at first (conditions applied manually); emanation templates and target auto-application were finicky.

## Decisions and Outcomes

- The party scouted before assaulting and bribed the urchin for intel rather than going in blind.
- They entered via the forced side door (triggering the trap) rather than the padlocked loading doors.
- They prioritized freeing Gertraud mid-combat.
- They killed all four loyalists (no prisoner taken for questioning), despite a GM hint to keep one alive.
- They donated the spare looted weapons and armor to the League, keeping only a crossbow, bolts, and the two magic trinkets.
- They accepted Rhokrag's riverboat-ambush mission.

- The loyalists somehow knew the warehouse's location; how is unknown.
