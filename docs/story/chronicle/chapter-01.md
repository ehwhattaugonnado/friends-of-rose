# Chapter 1 — The Aftermath of Battle

## Revelations & Lore

### The "God Stuff" and Restless Dead

Utto mentioned that the dead have been restless in the region since "all that god stuff fell from the sky" — some kind of major divine event that seems to have disturbed the natural order. This appears to be a known local phenomenon, not just a rumor. The zombie giant bats you fought at the **North Conerica River Crossing** may be a manifestation of this.

### "Friends of Rose"

The call-and-response used to enter the Hellbreakers' Saringallow headquarters: knock, say "we're some friends of Rose," and the reply is "any friend of Rose can find refuge here." Ethran told you this is the expected code, and you should use it if you ever need to bring someone else to the HQ.

### The Hellbreakers' Code of Conduct

Before your second mission, [**Rhokrag**](../../codex/people/rhokrag.md) spelled out how [**The Hellbreakers League**](../../codex/factions/the-hellbreakers-league.md) wants you to operate. The work ahead involves lawbreaking — raids and piracy — but the rules are: take only what's needed and never from people who can't afford to lose it; the opposition is mostly hired guards and mercenaries doing a job, not enemies to be slaughtered; and above all, keep killing to a minimum. The League needs the people of Isger to see them as protectors, not warmongers — and Cheliax will seize on any bloodshed to brand the Hellbreakers as violent marauders. "No one will miss a bootlicker or two," but a trail of bodies is to be avoided. Defend yourselves, but don't give the enemy that propaganda.

### Zombie Giant Bats (Undead)

The two massive bats that attacked you at the **North Conerica River Crossing** were undead, not living animals. Key things the party learned about them:
- Weak to vitality (healing) energy — Tainis's healing spells did double damage
- Resistant to mundane physical damage (slashing still worked but they were hardy)
- Immune or resistant to most mental and poison effects
- They have a void/rotting aura that deals void damage and can knock creatures unconscious
- Affected by the Slowed 1 condition — they could only take one action per turn

### The Warehouse Breach

The [**Everbloom Imports**](../../codex/places/everbloom-imports.md) warehouse was supposed to be a secret League location, but [**Chelaxian Loyalists**](../../codex/factions/chelaxian-loyalists.md) knew exactly where it was — they forced their way in, set a trap, and lay in wait. Neither [**Gertraud Mildenhall**](../../codex/people/gertraud-mildenhall.md) nor [**Rhokrag**](../../codex/people/rhokrag.md) could explain how they found it. Rhokrag was clearly worried by the security implications. With no captives taken, the question of who tipped off the loyalists, and how, is wide open.

## Open Questions

### Something happened to Gertraud

The party suspected that [**Gertraud Mildenhall**](../../codex/people/gertraud-mildenhall.md)'s disappearance from the [**Everbloom Imports**](../../codex/places/everbloom-imports.md) warehouse was not a simple delay. This was borne out: in Session 002 you found her captured and held by [**Chelaxian Loyalists**](../../codex/factions/chelaxian-loyalists.md) who had taken over the warehouse. You killed them and freed her. She is alive and back at headquarters.
**Raised:** Session 001 — **Resolved:** Session 002

**Resolved.**

### How did the loyalists find the warehouse?

With [**Gertraud Mildenhall**](../../codex/people/gertraud-mildenhall.md) rescued, two new questions hang over the party. First, the [**Everbloom Imports**](../../codex/places/everbloom-imports.md) warehouse was supposed to be secret, yet the [**Chelaxian Loyalists**](../../codex/factions/chelaxian-loyalists.md) knew right where to find it — nobody (not even [**Rhokrag**](../../codex/people/rhokrag.md)) could say how. Second, there were no supplies on the ship at all; the expected crates were never there, whether intercepted in transit or never loaded. The party hasn't settled on an explanation, but the implication of an informant or a leak inside the operation is now in the air.
**Raised:** Session 002

### The lich on Lake Encarthan

Utto mentioned hearing rumors of a lich living on Lake Encarthan, possibly connected to the regional undead unrest. None of you knew anything more — your Society checks all failed. This rumor is unconfirmed and unexplored.
**Raised:** Session 001
